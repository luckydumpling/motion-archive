The second volume in Michael Betancourt's Routledge trilogy on title sequence semiotics. Where the first book addresses text-image composition, this one focuses on audio-visual synchronisation — how sound and image combine to produce meaning in motion graphics, and what the semiotic implications of that relationship are.

For students working with sound in motion design, it provides a theoretical vocabulary that is otherwise hard to find in the literature.
