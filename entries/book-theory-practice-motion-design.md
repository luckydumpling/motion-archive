Edited by R. Brian Stone and Leah Wahlin, published by Routledge in 2018, this anthology covers motion design from both theoretical and professional perspectives. The structure moves from semiotics, communication theory and perception through to brand identity and interdisciplinary origins, with essays from academics and practitioners alongside interviews with Kyle Cooper, Karin Fong and Daniel Alenquer.

One of the few academic books to address motion design directly as a discipline, rather than as a subset of film, graphic design or interaction design.
