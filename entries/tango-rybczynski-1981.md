Zbigniew Rybczyński's _Tango_ is a pre-digital compositing work of extraordinary formal rigour. A single static room. Thirty-six characters, each assigned a looping action, are layered into the frame one by one using hand-painted cell mattes and optical printing — several hundred thousand exposures over seven months. The piece winner the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film in 1983.

The logic is one of accumulation. Each iteration adds a body, a loop, a layer, until the frame is saturated with simultaneous independent actions. The room cannot physically contain what is shown — off-screen space is fully exploited — yet the choreography holds. What makes this instructive for motion design students is that the underlying problem — managing layered, looping elements in a shared frame without losing spatial or temporal legibility — is precisely the problem that compositing and motion systems solve today. Rybczyński solved it by hand.
