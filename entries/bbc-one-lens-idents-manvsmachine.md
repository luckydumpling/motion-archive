ManvsMachine's BBC One idents solve a specific brief: incorporate the new BBC logo's circular form into a full identity system while connecting the channel to a wide cross-section of British life. The solution is the Venn diagram lens, a device that functions simultaneously as a compositional tool, a transition mechanism, and the brand mark itself. The same element does three jobs at once.

Each ident layers three distinct scenes shot in the same location using a motion control rig, the lens revealing different communities occupying the same space at different times. The system generates 28 executions from a single formal rule, and the motion design logic holds across all of them. It is a textbook example of constraint producing variety.
