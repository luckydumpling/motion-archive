Movement's rebrand for France 5, directed by Alphabetical Order, uses typography as its primary motion material. Letters and words do not illustrate content — they perform it. The system is built around typographic accumulation and dispersal, generating visual energy from the properties of language in motion rather than from narrative or representational imagery.

The color palette is precise and deliberate, reinforcing France 5's position within the France Télévisions family while maintaining its own distinct register. The identity demonstrates how a constrained typographic system can produce sufficient variety to sustain a full broadcast package without losing coherence.
